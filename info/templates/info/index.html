Swabra Namayanja

Hi! I’m Swabra Namayanja (or just Swabra).

I do Human-centered computing research at the intersection of accessibility, climate action, public transit, and the global south. Specifically, I investigate and design equitable transit and climate action interventions in diverse geographical contexts (i.e., North America & East Africa). I adapt qualitative methods to understand the lived experiences of research populations from diverse backgrounds (i.e., with a focus on the lived experiences of disabled people).

I design frameworks which stakeholders such as policymakers can leverage to nudge their transportation ecosystems towards equity.

I completed my bachelor degree in Software Engineering at the School of Computing & informatics technology, Makerere University. I was advised by Dr. Kamulegeya Grace. My research was funded by Makerere Student Project Fund.
